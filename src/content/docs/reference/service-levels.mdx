## Support Response Times

{/* TODO: confirm response times with team */}

Response times are measured from ticket creation to first meaningful response during business hours (Mon–Fri, 08:00–18:00 CET).

| Severity | Description | Basic | Standard 1000–2000 | Standard 3000–4000 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **P1 — Critical** | Service fully unavailable | Best-effort | 4 hours | 1 hour |
| **P2 — High** | Major feature degraded | Best-effort | 8 hours | 4 hours |
| **P3 — Medium** | Minor issue, workaround available | Best-effort | 2 business days | 1 business day |
| **P4 — Low** | General question or request | Best-effort | 5 business days | 3 business days |


## Planned Maintenance

- **Maintenance windows** — Scheduled maintenance may occur on weekends between 22:00–06:00 CET. Customers on Standard tiers are notified at least 5 business days in advance.
- **Emergency maintenance** — Critical security patches or urgent fixes may be applied outside scheduled windows with best-effort advance notice.
- **Impact** — Maintenance may cause brief service interruptions. We aim to keep downtime under 30 minutes per maintenance window.


## Incident Communication

- **Status page** — Real-time service health is available at the [Model Status](https://uptime.llmhub.t-systems.net/status/health) page.
- **Notifications** — Standard-tier customers receive email notifications for P1/P2 incidents and planned maintenance.
- **Post-incident reports** — Root cause analyses are shared for P1 incidents affecting Standard 3000+ customers.


## Escalation

For urgent issues or enterprise-specific SLA requirements:

- **Support portal** — Open a support ticket through the [T-Cloud Marketplace](https://marketplace.otc.t-systems.com/)
- **Direct contact** — Reach the AIFS team at [ai@t-systems.com](mailto:ai@t-systems.com)
- **Custom SLAs** — Enterprise customers with dedicated GPU resources can negotiate custom availability targets and support terms
